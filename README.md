# Retail Sales and Profit Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data from a Superstore dataset to uncover business insights related to sales performance, profitability, customer segments, product categories, and regional trends. The goal is to use data analytics techniques to identify key drivers of revenue and profit and provide recommendations that can support business decision-making.

## Business Problem

Retail businesses generate large amounts of transactional data every day. Understanding which products, regions, and customer segments contribute most to sales and profit is essential for improving performance and making informed strategic decisions. This analysis aims to answer important business questions such as:

* Which regions generate the highest sales?
* Which product categories are the most profitable?
* Which customer segments contribute the most revenue?
* How do discounts affect profitability?
* Which states represent the strongest markets?

## Dataset

The dataset contains 9,994 retail transactions and includes information on:

* Customer segments
* Product categories and sub-categories
* Sales and profit
* Discounts and quantities
* Geographic regions and states
* Shipping modes

## Project Objectives

The main objectives of this analysis are:

1. Perform data exploration and cleaning.
2. Analyze sales and profit across regions, categories, and customer segments.
3. Investigate the relationship between discounts and profitability.
4. Identify top-performing states and business areas.
5. Generate actionable recommendations based on the findings.

## Key Findings

* Total Sales: $2.30M
* Total Profit: $286K
* West region generated the highest sales.
* Technology was the most profitable product category.
* Consumer customers generated the largest share of revenue.
* Higher discounts were associated with lower profitability.

## Business Recommendations

* Increase investment in high-performing regions.
* Expand Technology product offerings.
* Strengthen engagement with Consumer customers.
* Review discount policies to protect profit margins.
